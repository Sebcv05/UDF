# Fixes Applied: 2025-11-11

## Issue 1: Early Parcels Skipping Thermal Breakup

### Problem
First few parcels injected at early timesteps persisted as large droplets throughout the simulation without undergoing thermal breakup, even though they had active bubbles and growing kb values.

### Root Cause
**File:** `src/spray_drop_distort_NH3.c`, lines 418-428

A premature expansion check was acting as a gatekeeper:
```c
if(old_parcel_cloud.radius[p_idx]> old_parcel_cloud.r_drop_0[p_idx]*1.5)
{
   old_parcel_cloud.thermal_breakup_flag[p_idx] = 1;
   old_parcel_cloud.tbt[p_idx] = 1;
   old_parcel_cloud.pbt[p_idx] = 0;
   old_parcel_cloud.thermal_breakup_flag[p_idx] = 6;
   continue;
}
```

**What was happening:**
1. Parcel injected at 293 K (subcooled relative to ambient 298 K)
2. Bubble nucleates and grows via RPE solver
3. kb (breakup criterion) increases: 0.0578 → 0.0580 (trending toward threshold)
4. BUT droplet radius doesn't expand to 1.5x original size
5. Code hits line 418 check, fails, and **continues to next parcel** (implicit fall-through)
6. Never reaches kb > threshold check at line 565
7. Parcel persists indefinitely

**Why this affected early parcels specifically:**
- Early parcels have less time to heat up from ambient gas
- Lower superheat → slower bubble growth → less droplet expansion
- Later parcels have warmed gas field → faster heating → more expansion → pass the 1.5x check

### Solution
**Commented out lines 418-428** in `spray_drop_distort_NH3.c`

The 1.5x expansion check was likely intended as a safety mechanism for extreme cases, but it was:
1. Incorrectly placed before the physics-based kb criterion
2. Using an arbitrary geometric threshold (1.5x) instead of the disturbance growth rate
3. Preventing parcels from ever reaching the proper breakup criterion

**The kb > kb_threshold check at line 565 should be the ONLY criterion for triggering thermal breakup.**

### Verification
After fix, expect to see:
- All parcels undergo thermal breakup when kb exceeds threshold
- No "orphan" large droplets persisting at late times
- kb values in tracked_parcel.csv approach and exceed threshold
- thermal_breakup_flag transitions from -1 → 3 (kb-triggered breakup)

## Additional Notes

### Why the 1.5x check existed
Likely added as a safeguard to trigger breakup if droplet expanded extremely (e.g., due to rapid bubble growth or numerical issues). However:
- Proper physics should handle this via kb criterion
- DGRE (Disturbance Growth Rate Equation) already accounts for bubble-driven instabilities
- Geometric threshold is less robust than rate-based criterion

### Design principle
**One criterion per physical mechanism:**
- Thermal breakup → kb > kb_threshold (disturbance growth rate)
- KH-RT breakup → separate routine
- Collision breakup → separate routine

Mixing geometric checks with rate-based criteria creates opportunities for bugs like this one.
